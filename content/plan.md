## Find your polling place

Your polling place is assigned by precinct and can change when district lines
are redrawn. Confirm yours before every election — don't rely on where you
voted last time.

Enter your address at your county's lookup tool to find where you vote on Election Day:

- [Fairfax County](https://www.fairfaxcounty.gov/myneighborhood/)
- [Loudoun County](https://www.loudoun.gov/2782/96522/Polling-Place-Lookup)

## Know your options

### Vote on Election Day

Polls are open **6 a.m. – 7 p.m.** in Virginia. If you're in line before 7 p.m.
you have the right to vote.

### Vote early in person

Virginia offers no-excuse early voting starting 45 days before Election Day at
your local general registrar's office or satellite locations. Hours vary by
locality — check your county's registrar website for the schedule.

### Vote by mail (absentee)

Any registered Virginia voter may request an absentee ballot — no excuse
required. Request one through the state portal and return it by mail or drop it
at a drop box by 7 p.m. on Election Day.

[Request an absentee ballot](https://vote.elections.virginia.gov/VoterInformation)

## What to bring

Virginia does not require photo ID to vote, but poll workers will ask for your
name and address. Bringing your voter registration card or a utility bill with
your current address can speed up the check-in process.
